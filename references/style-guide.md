# Instructure UX Writing Style Guide
Last updated: April 1, 2025 | Lisette Walberer

---

## About This Guide
This style guide is for the product design and marketing teams at Instructure. It includes writing guidelines that help us create a clear and consistent user experience.

---

## Voice and Tone

### Our Voice (Always Consistent)
Instructure's voice reflects our company values: Ownership, Relationships, Equality, Openness, Simplicity.

**Clear**
Prefer simple, straightforward language over industry jargon. Make dense information easier to understand and tough decisions easier to make.

**Confident**
Not obnoxious — not wishy-washy. We love what we do, we know the market, we make great edtech, and we sound like it.

**Conversational**
Use contractions. Acknowledge shared human experiences. Make jokes and quips. Empathize and commiserate.

---

### Our Tone (Shifts with Context)
Tone is our mood — it adapts to the user's needs and emotional state.

| Tone | What it means | What it avoids |
|---|---|---|
| Knowledgeable | Conveys expertise | Know-it-all, "actually..." |
| Encouraging | Inspires, supports, empowers | Patronizing |
| Sincere | Shows we hear and care | Schmaltz, manipulative emotion |
| Playful | Pop culture, wordplay, friendly winks | Snarky, childish, alienating |
| Direct | Gets to the point | Abrasive, skips transitions |

**Factors that determine tone:**
- Stage of user journey
- User emotions
- Space for content

## Writing for Everyone

### Plain Language

**Why it matters:** Accessibility (WCAG), SEO, reading behavior, business outcomes.

**Keep it short:**
- Word length: No more than 3 syllables
- Sentence length: No more than 25 words
- Paragraph length: No more than 150 words

**Use simple words:**

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Help | Assistance |
| Get | Access |
| Get rid of | Eliminate |
| Help | Facilitate |
| Ask | Request |
| Need | Require |
| To | In order to |
| If | In the event that |
| About | In regard to |
| Pay | Make a payment |

**Other plain language rules:**
- Be conversational — read copy out loud to check
- Use contractions (avoid conditional ones: could've, should've, would've)
- Use active voice by default
- Avoid hidden verbs (verbs turned into nouns)
- Use present tense
- Use second-person ("your," "yours")
- Avoid jargon unless the audience definitely knows it
- Use headers to add structure
- Use lists for scannable content

**Avoid hidden verbs:**

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Except for | With the exception of |
| Applies to | Is applicable to |
| Review | Leave a review |

---

### Inclusive Language

**General guidance:**
- Only refer to someone's identity when it's relevant
- Ask people how they prefer to be referred to when possible
- Use "they/them/their" when pronoun is unknown
- Default to person-first language for disabilities (unless specified otherwise)
- Default to identity-first language for race (unless specified otherwise)
- Avoid language, metaphors, and idioms that exclude or assume a certain class, culture, ability, or age
- Avoid culturally insensitive slang

**Disabilities:**

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Person with a disability | Disabled person |
| Person without a disability | Able-bodied person |
| Has a disability | Suffers from a disability |
| Disabled | Differently-abled |
| Overlooked spot | Blind spot |
| Show/view more | See more |
| Unbelievable | Crazy/insane/dumb |
| Feature off/deactivated | Feature disabled |
| In the menu, before, next, [specific language] | On the left, above, below, here |

**Race and Culture:**

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Black person | Person who is black |
| Underrepresented groups | Minorities |
| Legacied | Grandfathered |
| It's been a while | Long time no see |
| No | No can do |
| Authority/expert | Guru |
| Meeting | Powwow |
| Let's be transparent | Let's open the kimono |

**Gender:**

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| LGBTQ+ | Homosexual |
| Sexual orientation | Sexual preference |
| Pronouns | Preferred pronouns |
| Women and men | Female and male |
| Hey everyone | Hey guys |
| Everyone | Ladies and gentlemen |
| Businessperson | Businessman |
| Staffed/managed | Manned |
| Workforce | Manpower |
| Parental leave | Maternity/paternity leave |

## Grammar and Mechanics

### Acronyms
Spell out unusual acronyms on first use with the acronym in parentheses. Use only the acronym after that. Exception: skip if the acronym is already commonly known by users.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| We use a large language model (LLM) to generate ideas. | We use an LLM to generate ideas. |

For time zones, use two-letter format with a space before the acronym.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| 8 am PT | 8 am PST |

---

### Active vs. Passive Voice

**Default to active voice.**

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| We'll find the right person to help you. | The right person can be found to help you. |

**Use passive voice when:**
- Avoiding blaming the user or system
- Putting users in the spotlight instead of Instructure

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| The password requirements weren't met. | You didn't meet the password requirements. |
| Your password was updated. | We updated your password. |

---

### Alt Text
Write alt text for: images/icons/buttons/graphics, charts/diagrams, audio/video files, form controls and UI components.

**Rules:**
- Don't write alt text for purely decorative content
- Provide a short, clear description of the information or function
- Don't include "image," "icon," or "picture" — screen readers announce this automatically

---

### Ampersands (&)
Use in headings and CTAs only when there's a hard character limit. Never in full sentences or after commas.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| CTA button: Book & pay | Body text: We'll help you find, book, & pay a pro. |

---

### Asterisks (*)
Use to indicate required fields.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Enter password* | Enter password (required) |

---

### Capitalization

**Title case** (capitalize all major words):
- Buttons, headers, sub-headers, menus, navigation, tabs, labels, status indicators, breadcrumbs

**Sentence case** (capitalize first word + proper nouns only):
- Tooltips, placeholder text, body text, helper text, checkboxes, radio buttons, alerts

---

### Colons
Use to introduce bulleted lists, numbered lists, and inline lists. Don't use to introduce radio buttons or checkboxes. Don't capitalize the first word after a colon in a sentence.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Tip: double-check your spelling. | Tip: Double-check your spelling. |

---

### Contractions
Use contractions to sound conversational and save space. Avoid conditional contractions (could've, should've, would've).

---

### Dashes

**Hyphen (-):** Connect two or more words that work together. Don't hyphenate "very" or most -ly words.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Auto-pay | Autopay or Auto pay |
| Higher-intent customers | Higher intent customers |

**En dash (–):** Use for ranges. No spaces on either side.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| 8–10 am | 8 – 10 am |

**Em dash (—):** Use to separate phrases or add an aside. Spaces on either side.

Uses: break in a sentence, separating a noun from the rest, open date ranges, emphasizing a point.

---

### Ellipses
Use only for truncation.

---

### Emojis
Use rarely and only when the tone is celebratory. Never more than once per page. Never to replace a word.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| You finished the assignment. 🎉 | You finished an 📝. |

---

### Exclamation Marks (!)
Use only in moments of celebration. Never more than once per page.

---

### First vs. Third Person
Default to first-person plural ("we") when referencing Instructure in the product.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| We're working on a solution. | Instructure is working on a solution. |

Use third person for: brand copy, legal copy, help center content, references to customers and products.

---

### Headers
Use contextual, descriptive headers. Users should skim headers and understand the page.

- H1: page title — one per page only
- H2: second-level headings
- H3: third-level headings — use sparingly
- Don't skip heading levels
- Use title case for all headers

---

### Links
Link text should specifically describe the destination. Don't link punctuation at the end of a sentence.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Learn more about using inclusive language. | Click here. |

---

### Lists
- Use a colon to introduce items
- Limit to six items or less
- Order items logically
- Keep items similar in length
- Don't end items with commas
- Use numbered lists when order matters

---

### Numbers

**Spell out when:**
- Used in a sentence
- De-emphasizing a figure
- Very large numbers (combine numeral + word: "4 billion")

**Use numerals for:**
- Numbers 100 and up
- Time, tables, equations, ranges, dates, percentages
- Emphasizing a figure

**Dates:**
- Spell out whenever possible: February 7, 2025
- Don't use two digits for single-digit dates
- Numerical format: 02/07/2025 (MM/DD/YYYY)
- Ranges within one year: Feb 21–Feb 22, 2025
- Ranges across years: include year in both dates

**Phone numbers:** Use xxx-xxx-xxxx format. Link when possible.

**Time:**
- Lowercase "am" or "pm" with a space before
- Drop :00 for on-the-hour times
- Use "to" for ranges when space allows, otherwise en dash
- Omit first am/pm if range is within the same part of day
- Use two-letter time zone abbreviation

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| 8 am | 8:00a.m. |
| 8–10 am | 8 am – 10 am |
| 8 am PT | 8 am EST |

---

### Oxford Comma
Always use.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Collaborate, innovate, and succeed. | Collaborate, innovate and succeed. |

---

### Parentheses
Use to provide context, counts, or introduce abbreviations. Place period outside the closing parenthesis unless the entire sentence is inside.

---

### Periods
Use only at the end of complete sentences.

**Never use periods in:**
- Buttons
- Headers, labels, or titles
- Alerts (unless full sentences)
- Month, day, or time abbreviations
- Short, direct, standalone phrases

---

### Semicolons
Never use. Use an em dash or rewrite the sentence.

---

### Text Formatting
- **Bold:** Use to emphasize a point or reference navigation
- *Italics:* Not used in product (marketing/longform only)
- Underline: Links only
- Strikethrough: Never — not accessible
- Vertical bars (|): Use to separate metadata, not bullets

## Common Content

### Emails
Keep under 150 words total. One topic per paragraph, max four lines (~50 words).

- **Subject lines:** Under 10 words / 40 characters. No punctuation. Lead with the most important info.
- **Pre-headers:** Under 10 words / 40 characters. Complete sentence with punctuation. Reinforces or continues the subject line.
- **Headers:** Optional. Repeat or expand the subject line. One line ideally.
- **Body:** Concise. Use subheaders or bullets for complex info. Max three lines per paragraph.
- **CTAs:** Always include at least one. Subject line + header + CTA should tell the full story.
- **Footers:** Optional secondary CTA. Always include business address, unsubscribe link, app download links.

---

### Push and SMS Notifications
Under 90 characters. Write for a small phone screen.

Send push first — only send SMS if notifications are off.

- **Headers:** One line. Main message. Optional emoji for celebratory tone.
- **Body:** No more than three lines. Indicate where the user will go if they tap through.

---

### Calls-to-Action (Buttons and Links)
- Buttons: sentence case, four words or less
- Links: sentence case, eight words or less
- Exception: use title case only when containing a proper noun

**4 guidelines (Nielsen Norman Group):**
- **Specific:** Start with strong, actionable verbs. Avoid "click."
- **Sincere:** Accurately describe the destination.
- **Substantial:** Understandable without surrounding context.
- **Succinct:** Cut unnecessary words (articles, adjectives).

Use [action verb] + [noun] pattern unless following a common pattern like "Done" or "Continue."

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| View policy details | Learn more |
| Start free trial | Try it now! |
| Set reminder | Set a reminder |

---

### Errors and Alerts
Explain what happened and what to do next. Avoid generic language.

**Rules:**
- Adjust tone — never use an exclamation point
- Use passive voice to avoid placing blame
- Don't make jokes
- Avoid technical jargon (like "Error 404")

**Headers:** One line, max 10 words. User should be able to resolve the error from header + CTA alone.

**Body:** Complete sentences, max two lines. Provide context on why the error occurred.

**CTAs:** Four words or less, no punctuation. Direct users to resolve on their own when possible.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| The payment failed. Double-check your information and try again. | AUTH ERROR: We weren't able to process your payment. |
| Incorrect password | You entered the wrong password. |
